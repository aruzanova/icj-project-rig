Extended List

Explore a detailed list of endangered and threatened species from the extended dataset, featuring their habitats and behaviors.

  • White-faced ibis

    Description: This species is found in specific counties during migration, favoring freshwater marshes, sloughs, rice fields, and occasionally brackish or saltwater habitats, with nesting in marshes, low trees, reeds, or floating mats. Its presence varies by season and is currently limited to near-coastal rookeries in hog-wallow prairies.

    Scientific Name: Plegadis chihi

    Taxon: Birds

  • Willet

    Description: This species inhabits marshes, tidal mudflats, beaches, lake margins, mangroves, tidal channels, river mouths, coastal lagoons, shores, and occasionally open grasslands.

    Scientific Name: Tringa semipalmata

    Taxon: Birds

  • Sanderling

    Description: During nonbreeding, this species primarily occupies sandy beaches, mudflats, lake or river shores, and exposed reefs, often resting on upper beaches or salt pond dikes.

    Scientific Name: Calidris alba

    Taxon: Birds

  • Franklin's gull

    Description: This species migrates through Texas in spring and fall, does not breed there, and is rarely seen in winter, typically as single individuals along the Gulf coast. During migration, it roosts at wetlands, lake shores, or islands overnight after flying during daylight hours.

    Scientific Name: Leucophaeus pipixcan

    Taxon: Birds

  • Yellow-billed cuckoo

    Description: In Texas, the Western Distinct Population Segment of this species breeds in riparian woodlands below 6,000 feet in the Trans Pecos region, arriving in May and wintering in South America. Threats include hydrologic changes preventing cottonwood and willow regeneration and livestock impacts on sapling trees.

    Scientific Name: Coccyzus americanus

    Taxon: Birds

  • Common nighthawk

    Description: Common Nighthawks nest in diverse habitats, from natural areas like dunes, prairies, and forests to urban flat gravel rooftops, though the shift to rubberized roofs has reduced suitable nesting sites.

    Scientific Name: Chordeiles minor

    Taxon: Birds

  • Bank swallow

    Description: Bank Swallows inhabit low areas near water with vertical cliffs or banks for colonial nesting, often using natural bluffs or human-made sites like quarries and road cuts. They forage in open areas, avoiding tree-covered landscapes.

    Scientific Name: Riparia riparia

    Taxon: Birds

  • Loggerhead shrike

    Description: Loggerhead Shrikes prefer open habitats with short vegetation and scattered shrubs or low trees, including fields, pastures, orchards, and desert scrublands, often near roadsides, fence lines, or utility poles.

    Scientific Name: Lanius ludovicianus

    Taxon: Birds

  • Wilson's warbler

    Description: Wilsonâ€™s Warblers rely on streamside forests and scrub for feeding during migration and use diverse habitats, from lowland thickets to high-elevation cloud forests, during the nonbreeding season.

    Scientific Name: Cardellina pusilla

    Taxon: Birds

  • Brewer's blackbird

    Description: This species inhabits shrubby areas near water, riparian woodlands, aspen parklands, cultivated lands, marshes, and human habitation, extending to pastures and fields during migration and winter.

    Scientific Name: Euphagus cyanocephalus

    Taxon: Birds

  • Hoary bat

    Description: Hoary bats are migratory, high-flying bats found statewide, with females migrating to Mexico in winter while males may stay in Texas year-round. They roost in forest foliage but are also found in unforested areas and deserts, often flying over water and open spaces.

    Scientific Name: Lasiurus cinereus

    Taxon: Mammals

  • Mountain lion

    Description: This species is a generalist, inhabiting diverse habitats across the state but is most commonly found in rugged mountains and riparian zones.

    Scientific Name: Puma concolor

    Taxon: Mammals

  • Northern bobwhite

    Description: This species thrives in various vegetation types, especially early successional stages, including croplands, grasslands, pastures, fallow fields, rangelands, open pinelands, mixed forests, and habitat mosaics.

    Scientific Name: Colinus virginianus

    Taxon: Birds

  • Texas horned lizard

    Description: This species inhabits open areas with sparse vegetation, such as grasslands, prairies, and scrub, using soil burrows or rodent burrows for shelter, typically below 6,000 feet and mainly below the pinyon-juniper zone in the Big Bend region.

    Scientific Name: Phrynosoma cornutum

    Taxon: Reptiles

  • Western box turtle

    Description: Western box turtles primarily inhabit grasslands, pastures, and woodlands, occasionally entering shallow water, and shelter by burrowing into soil or using existing burrows.

    Scientific Name: Terrapene ornata

    Taxon: Reptiles

  • Bald eagle

    Description: Ornate and western box turtles inhabit prairies, pastures, fields, sandhills, and open woodlands, occasionally entering shallow streams or pools. They seek shelter by burrowing into soil or using burrows made by other species.

    Scientific Name: Haliaeetus leucocephalus

    Taxon: Birds

  • Tricolored bat

    Description: This species relies on forests, woodlands, riparian areas, and caves, with caves being particularly crucial.

    Scientific Name: Perimyotis subflavus

    Taxon: Mammals

  • Snowy plover

    Description: Algal flats, valued for their inaccessibility and year-round availability, are the highest quality habitat for this species, with larger areas supporting larger populations. Once an uncommon breeder in the Panhandle, it is now a potential migrant and winters along the coast.

    Scientific Name: Charadrius nivosus

    Taxon: Birds

  • Western burrowing owl

    Description: This species inhabits open grasslands like prairies, plains, and savannas, occasionally using open areas near human habitation or airports, and relies on abandoned burrows for nesting and roosting.

    Scientific Name: Athene cunicularia hypugaea

    Taxon: Birds

  • Eastern spotted skunk

    Description: This generalist species occupies open fields, prairies, croplands, and woodlands, favoring wooded, brushy areas and tallgrass prairies. Subspecies S.p. interrupta prefers rocky canyons and outcrops in wooded and tallgrass prairie regions.

    Scientific Name: Spilogale putorius

    Taxon: Mammals
